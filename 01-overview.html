Introduction to graph databases

Graphs consist of nodes and relationships, forming a network-like data structure. Similar to how the neurons of the brain are connected, graph databases excel at making sense of and storing any kind of connected data.

These connections can be queried with high performance, often thousands of times faster than a relational database. This is done using the Cypher graph query language.
